# Tommy Coconut Experiences Page - Narrative Design

## 1. PAGE STRUCTURE RECOMMENDATION

### Hero Section Architecture
- **Visual**: Full-width video loop or carousel showing glimpses of all three experiences
- **Headline Hierarchy**: Primary focus on emotional transformation, secondary on inclusion
- **Immediate Emotional Hook**: Start with the feeling, not the features

### Section Flow & Narrative Arc
1. **Opening Promise** (Hero): The transformation awaiting them
2. **The Invitation** (Intro): Framing these as your "greatest hits" moments
3. **Experience 1 - Ocean Adventure**: Start with highest energy/adventure
4. **Experience 2 - Community Connection**: Build social warmth
5. **Experience 3 - Nature Intimacy**: End with peaceful, grounding experience
6. **The Convergence** (Closing): Tie all three into one transformative stay
7. **Booking Gateway**: Natural progression to reservation

### CTA Placement Strategy
- **Primary CTA**: After hero, subtle "Reserve Your Adventures"
- **Experience CTAs**: Small "Learn More" expansions for logistics
- **Final CTA**: Strong emotional close with booking emphasis
- **Floating Widget**: Persistent but non-intrusive booking access

## 4. TONE GUIDELINES

### Preview vs. Detail Balance
- **70% Emotional Preview**: Paint the feeling and transformation
- **30% Reassuring Detail**: Enough logistics to feel real and planned
- Never feel like a brochure—always feel like a friend's enthusiastic recommendation

### Excitement vs. Reassurance Mix
- Lead with excitement and possibility
- Follow with gentle reassurance about logistics
- Never defend or justify—simply celebrate what's included
- Use "already yours" and "included in your stay" naturally, not defensively

### Conveying "Included" Without Price Justification
- Frame as "signature experiences" not "included amenities"
- Use language like "part of your story," "waiting for you," "already yours"
- Position as carefully curated, not bulk packaged
- Emphasize personal delivery (Mike, Happy, Lucky) over commodity

## IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

### Emotional Through-Line
The consistent thread: "You're not a tourist here—you're family being shown the treasures we love most."

### Visual Recommendations
- Use candid, joy-filled photos over staged shots
- Show real guests with Mike, at BBQ, with Happy and Lucky
- Include videos if possible—movement conveys energy
- Feature diverse families to show universal appeal

### Interactive Elements
- Consider expandable sections for logistics details
- Photo galleries for each experience
- Guest story carousel
- Simple booking widget integration

### SEO Considerations
- Target: "Curacao private experiences," "included activities Curacao," "Curacao family adventures"
- Use natural language that matches search intent
- Include location markers (Spanish Water, salt flats, etc.)

This narrative design transforms three included activities into an emotional journey that visitors can already feel themselves experiencing. It's not about justifying value—it's about creating inevitable desire.
