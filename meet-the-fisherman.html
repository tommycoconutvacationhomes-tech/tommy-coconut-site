Meet the Fisherman

Why Captain Emilio's Tuna is the Only One We Serve

Words by Tommy Coconut

Every traveler has felt the sting of the "Tourist Trap Restaurant." The glossy menu, the mediocre food, the feeling that you've just paid a premium for something completely inauthentic.

At Tommy Coconut, we have a simple philosophy: if you want the best fish, you don't go to a restaurant. You go to the fisherman.

Let us introduce you to a local legend: Captain Emilio.

He has been reading the waters of Curaçao for over 40 years. He doesn't use fancy electronics; he follows the birds and the currents. He speaks the language of the sea. And he is the only person we trust to provide the fresh, line-caught tuna for our family and our guests.

Building trust, one handshake at a time - our partnership with Captain Emilio spans decades

Captain Emilio's weathered hands tell the story of four decades spent hauling nets and reading weather patterns that most people never notice. When he points to a patch of seemingly empty ocean and says "the tuna are there," he's never wrong. This isn't luck—it's mastery earned through tens of thousands of hours on these waters.

Every morning before sunrise, while most of the island sleeps, Captain Emilio is already preparing his lines. His boat, weathered but immaculately maintained, cuts through the pre-dawn darkness guided by knowledge passed down through generations of fishermen. By the time the sun paints the Caribbean sky in shades of gold and coral, he's already found his spot.

This is about more than just fresh fish. It's about a promise.

It's the promise that when you're on our sunset cruise, the sashimi you're eating was caught that very morning, by a man who loves laman. It's the promise that your experience is woven into the real, authentic fabric of the island. It's the difference between being a tourist and feeling like you belong.

From Captain Emilio's morning catch to your sunset cruise - the taste of authentic Curaçao

When Captain Emilio's tuna arrives at our dock, still glistening with seawater, there's a moment of pure satisfaction that runs through our entire team. We know that in a few hours, our guests will taste not just exceptional fish, but a piece of Curaçao's soul. They'll taste the salt air of dawn, the pride of a master craftsman, and the care of a family that refuses to compromise.

This relationship didn't happen overnight. It was built through years of early morning conversations on the dock, through Captain Emilio teaching us to read the signs that predict a good fishing day, through our commitment to paying fair prices for exceptional quality. It's a partnership built on mutual respect—we respect his expertise, and he respects our promise to our guests.

This is what we mean when we say "we handle the details."

It's not just about the big things like the villa and the vehicle. It's about knowing the right people, building real relationships, and curating an experience so authentic, you can taste it.

When you bite into that perfectly prepared sashimi as the Caribbean sun sets behind you, you're not just enjoying dinner—you're experiencing the result of a 40-year friendship between a master fisherman and a family committed to authentic hospitality. You're tasting the difference between a vacation and a homecoming.

This is our commitment. This is the Dushi Life.
